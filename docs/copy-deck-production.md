This September 24-26, for the seventh anniversary of  Robert Hunter’s passing, we’ll honor the lyricist and poet in Portland, Oregon.

Join us as we bring together a community of “Hunter heads” through a series of curated events that explore what’s been sung on repeat—while we sing all that is still unsung.   We’ll pay tribute to the great Robert Hunter through poetry readings, deep dives into his creative influences, rousing live music, and intimate reflections from songwriters.

When one speaks the name “Robert Hunter,” it rings sweetly to those whose lives were shaped by his words. Robert Hunter. Grateful Dead lyricist.  Poet,  muse amplifier, maker of the new American Songbag. Enigma to many, troubadour to all. Though he lived a life opaque to us, he nevertheless intimately alchemized the hearts of deadheads—shaping its culture into a national treasure. His songs are responsible for millions of life milestones, business names, lovers and sordid heartbreaks. His words charged internal illuminations, responsible for a spectrum of Americana to personal ecstatics.

ABOUT
Your Hosts

Katherine Factor is an editor, book coach, and author of four Choose Your Own Adventures. Her debut poetry book, A Sybil Society, won the Interim Poetics Test Site Poetry Prize. She’s a graduate of the Iowa Writers’ Workshop and has appeared at the Oregon Country Fair, Azoth space, with the band Catal Hyuk, and at the University of San Diego, Miami Book Festival, Mission Creek Music & Arts Festival, the Brautigan Library, Literary Arts, Skylight Books, Books Are Magic, and in libraries and schools across America. Her poems can be found online, in print, and on KBOO and WFMU radio shows.

Darrin Daniel

Darrin  Daniel is the publisher of Cityful Press and the poetry editor for the arts & literary magazine, Emergency Horse. He was a student of Allen Ginsberg, Harry Smith, and Robert Hunter while studying poetry and literature at Naropa University. Daniel was raised in Eugene, Oregon. He’s authored, edited, and/or published on Harry Smith (Think of the Self Speaking: Selected Interviews of Harry Smith),  Stan Brakhage, Dark Music by Gordon Ball, and Robert Hunter’s poetry chapbook, Infinity Minus Eleven. Daniel interviewed Robert Hunter in 1993 and later hosted a reading of his poems at the Boulder Bookstore in July 1994.
